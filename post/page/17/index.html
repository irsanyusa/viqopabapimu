Born in Amarkot, now in modern-day Pakistan, he was the son of Emperor Humayun and Hamida Banu Begum. Ascending to the throne as a teenager, Akbar expanded Mughal control across the Indian subcontinent and is remembered for his innovative administrative policies, military conquests, and encouragement of cultural fusion.
